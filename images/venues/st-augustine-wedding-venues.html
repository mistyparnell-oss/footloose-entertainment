St. Augustine draws couples for a reason — few cities in Florida combine historic architecture, waterfront views, and walkable downtown charm the way America's oldest city does. As a wedding DJ company that regularly works venues throughout St. Augustine, we get asked for venue recommendations constantly, and the same names come up again and again. Here are eighteen of them, with what to know about each and how we think about entertainment logistics for every one of them.

01

The White Room

St. Augustine's only private waterfront venue in the historic district, built in 1888, with three distinct spaces — the Grand Ballroom, The Loft & Rooftop, and the more intimate Villa Blanca. Consistently one of the most awarded venues on The Knot and WeddingWire. Many couples use more than one of its three spaces in the same day — a rooftop ceremony followed by a ballroom reception is a common combination, which is part of why it books out well over a year in advance for peak season dates.

whiteroomweddings.com·(904) 824-9056

Read our full White Room guide →
02

Treasury on the Plaza

A converted 1920s bank building in the heart of downtown, known for The Vault — the original bank vault, now a striking bar and photo backdrop. Books exclusive-use weddings of 40–175 guests only, with three package tiers ranging from fully custom to fully bundled. The venue itself notes it works best for weddings in the 75–150 guest range, large enough to feel celebratory without the historic rooms feeling empty.

treasuryontheplaza.com·(904) 217-0077

Read our full Treasury on the Plaza guide →
03

Casa Monica Resort

A landmark Moorish Revival hotel in downtown St. Augustine, offering grand indoor ballroom space for up to roughly 250 guests alongside full-service resort amenities — a strong choice for couples who want a historic feel with hotel-level catering and accommodations in one building. Because it operates as a full hotel, out-of-town guests can book rooms on-site, which simplifies logistics for destination weddings drawing family from outside Northeast Florida.

kesslercollection.com/casa-monica·(904) 827-1888

04

The Legacy at World Golf Village

A ballroom-style venue in World Golf Village offering polished, full-service wedding packages away from the downtown historic district — a good fit for couples who want a more traditional ballroom feel with easier parking and access. Its location off I-95 also makes it a practical choice for guests driving in from Jacksonville, Orlando, or further afield, without the parking and one-way-street logistics of downtown St. Augustine.

golfwgv.com/weddings-events·(904) 907-4767

05

The Carriage House

A genuine barn venue — a climate-controlled, roughly 2,000-square-foot barn with a carriage-wheel chandelier and barn-door gates, seating around 130 guests with room for 150–160 at a cocktail-style reception. Farmhouse tables and string-lit outdoor space round it out for couples who want a real rustic feel rather than a hotel ballroom dressed up to look rustic.

houseofassemblyevents.com

06

The Lightner Museum

Housed in the former Alcazar Hotel, a Gilded Age resort built in 1888, the Lightner Museum offers arched walkways, an elegant courtyard, and a grand indoor space with a level of architectural detail few other downtown venues can match. It accommodates an intimate gathering of around 40 guests up to a larger celebration of roughly 225, making it flexible for both small and mid-size weddings right in the heart of the historic district.

weddings.lightnermuseum.org·(904) 217-0077

07

The Magnolia Room at Ponce de Leon's Fountain of Youth

A waterfront venue set within one of St. Augustine's most historically significant properties, with roughly 2,500 square feet of enclosed reception space plus nearly 2,800 square feet of outdoor patios overlooking the Matanzas River — seating up to about 170 guests, or 300 for a standing reception. The grounds are also home to a resident flock of peacocks, and because the venue doesn't have in-house catering, couples choose from a preferred vendor list rather than a single house caterer.

fountainofyouthflorida.com·(904) 342-8388

08

The Palencia Club

A 33,000-square-foot Mediterranean-style clubhouse accommodating up to 180 guests for a cocktail reception or 150 for seated dining and dancing — a golf-club setting a bit outside the downtown core. Its Mediterranean architecture and golf-course backdrop give it a different visual identity from both the historic downtown venues and the more traditional ballroom options at World Golf Village.

palenciaclub.com·(904) 599-9040

09

Chandler Oaks Barn

An elegant white barn on 20 secluded acres about 15–20 minutes northwest of downtown, accommodating up to 200 guests for ceremony and reception. It's a strong fit for couples who want a barn setting with a more polished, upscale finish than a rough-hewn rustic barn.

chandleroaksbarn.com·(904) 339-4129

10

The River House

A waterfront venue in downtown St. Augustine with a Grand Ballroom seating up to 300, an intimate Library space, and outdoor terraces overlooking the water — giving couples a downtown location with more room to scale up than some of the historic buildings nearby.

riverhouseevents.com·(904) 826-6210

11

Ancient City Farmstead

A 1,500-acre property just outside the historic district with two distinct spaces — the Grand Cypress Barn, seating up to 300, and the more intimate Farmhouse, which seats 50 for a meal or up to 100 for a cocktail-style ceremony. A good option for couples who want a genuine rural setting without leaving the St. Augustine area entirely.

ancientcityfarmstead.com

12

Kelly Farm Events

A 95-acre rustic barn venue about 25 minutes from downtown St. Augustine, with an indoor barn space and multiple outdoor ceremony locations, including gazebo and sunset patio settings — a genuinely rural option for couples planning a barn wedding further from the historic district.

kellyfarmevents.com·(904) 392-5318

13

The Venue at Hastings

A restored 1927 building — originally the Hastings Motor Company, then Stanton Motors Ford dealership — about 30 minutes southwest of downtown St. Augustine in the small agricultural town of Hastings. It scales from intimate gatherings up to 350+ guests, making it one of the larger-capacity options on this list despite its small-town setting.

@thevenuehastings·(904) 806-4274

14

Embassy Suites by Hilton St. Augustine Beach Oceanfront Resort

An oceanfront all-suite resort in St. Augustine Beach with the 5,200-square-foot Anastasia Ballroom hosting up to 300 for a seated reception or splitting into three smaller breakout rooms, plus the smaller Coquina Room and Surf Crest Boardroom for more intimate gatherings. A full-service hotel option for couples who want on-site guest rooms and oceanfront views without leaving the property.

hilton.com·(904) 461-9004

15

Guy Harvey Resort St. Augustine Beach

An oceanfront resort in St. Augustine Beach with a 2,800-square-foot ballroom accommodating up to 150 guests, plus the smaller Nautilus Ballroom (up to 120, with its own outdoor terrace) and the intimate Fairwinds Reception Room (up to 40). Beach ceremonies are available with a permit for parties over fifteen — a mid-size, beach-adjacent option a bit more relaxed than a full convention-style resort.

guyharveyresortstaugustinebeach.com·(904) 471-2555

16

Hyatt Place St. Augustine / Vilano Beach

A newer hotel property in Vilano Beach with roughly 20,000 square feet of indoor and outdoor event space across six distinct areas, headlined by a 7,200-square-foot ballroom, with smaller rooms ranging from about 90 to 190 guests for more intimate events. Its scale and flexibility make it one of the larger-capacity hotel options in the St. Augustine area, north of the historic downtown core.

hyatt.com·(904) 295-1111

17

Renaissance St. Augustine Historic Downtown Hotel

A full-service Marriott property right in the historic downtown district, with the San Marco Ballroom seating up to 370 guests and the smaller Barcelona Room (up to 200) and Kirkside Room (up to 32) for rehearsal dinners or more intimate receptions. Combines downtown walkability with the on-site guest rooms and banquet infrastructure of a full hotel, which some of the older historic buildings on this list don't offer.

marriott.com·(904) 824-4457

18

World Golf Village Renaissance St. Augustine Resort

A large-scale Marriott resort in World Golf Village with roughly 45,000 square feet of event space across 16 rooms, including a 26,400-square-foot indoor ballroom and the outdoor La Terrazza space with a gazebo and fountain. By far the highest-capacity venue in this roundup, making it a strong fit for large weddings that need one property to handle both a big guest list and out-of-town hotel rooms.

marriott.com·(904) 940-8000

Choosing Among These Venues

With eighteen very different properties on this list, the right fit usually comes down to three questions: how many guests you're inviting, whether you want a downtown historic feel, a traditional ballroom or clubhouse setting, a full-service hotel or resort, or a rustic barn/farm setting further from downtown, and how much you want your out-of-town guests to be able to walk to dinner, drinks, and their hotel without needing a car.

Downtown venues like The White Room, Treasury on the Plaza, Casa Monica, and the Lightner Museum put you in the middle of St. Augustine's historic district — walkable, photogenic, and convenient for guests staying downtown, but also more constrained on load-in logistics, parking, and noise ordinances that come with operating inside a historic city center. Venues like The Legacy and The Palencia Club sit outside the downtown core, trading some of that historic-district charm for easier parking and more flexible layouts. A third group — Chandler Oaks Barn, Ancient City Farmstead, Kelly Farm Events, and The Venue at Hastings — moves further out still, into genuinely rural settings for couples who want open land and barn architecture rather than a downtown building. A fourth group of full-service hotels and resorts — Embassy Suites, Guy Harvey Resort, Hyatt Place Vilano Beach, the Renaissance Historic Downtown, and the World Golf Village Renaissance — gives couples on-site guest rooms and banquet infrastructure in one property, which matters most for weddings with a lot of out-of-town guests.

Guest count matters too. Treasury on the Plaza's 40–175 range and The Palencia Club's 150–180 range both work well for mid-size weddings, while The White Room's ability to flex from an intimate Villa Blanca gathering up to a 400-plus guest ballroom event makes it one of the few downtown venues that can comfortably handle either end of that spectrum. For larger guest lists, the barn venues also scale well — both Chandler Oaks Barn and Ancient City Farmstead's Grand Cypress Barn accommodate up to 300 guests. The World Golf Village Renaissance is the largest-capacity venue on this entire list by a wide margin, with its 26,400-square-foot ballroom, while Embassy Suites and the Renaissance Historic Downtown both comfortably handle 300–370 guests. If you're planning a wedding of 200 or more guests, it's worth confirming capacity early — several of the historic downtown buildings on this list simply weren't built to hold large modern guest lists.

Finally, think about season. St. Augustine's outdoor and waterfront spaces — the rooftop at The White Room, the gardens at The Carriage House, the riverfront at The Magnolia Room — are at their best in the cooler months and can turn uncomfortably hot and humid in the peak of a Florida summer. Indoor venues like the Lightner Museum and The Legacy sidestep that issue entirely, which is worth weighing if you're set on a summer wedding date.

Booking timelines vary by venue, but the most in-demand downtown properties — The White Room and Treasury on the Plaza in particular — regularly book popular fall and spring Saturdays well over a year in advance. If you have a specific date in mind and it falls during peak wedding season (roughly October through April in Northeast Florida, with January through March the busiest stretch), it's worth reaching out to your top choices as early as possible, and having a backup venue or two in mind in case your first pick is already booked. Once your venue is locked in, lining up entertainment early matters too — popular St. Augustine wedding dates fill DJ calendars just as fast as they fill venue calendars.

Planning entertainment across these venues: downtown historic buildings like The White Room and Treasury on the Plaza have fixed architecture and specific load-in routes, while golf clubhouse venues like The Legacy and The Palencia Club tend to have more flexible, purpose-built event space. As a Jacksonville and St. Augustine wedding DJ company, we plan sound and setup around whichever venue you book rather than a one-size-fits-all approach.

That familiarity extends across nearly every venue on this list — we've worked load-ins through downtown's narrow historic streets, outdoor lawn ceremonies in the middle of a Florida summer, and multi-room transitions at venues that use two or three spaces in a single day.
