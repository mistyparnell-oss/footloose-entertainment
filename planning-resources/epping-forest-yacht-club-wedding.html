About Epping Forest Yacht & Country Club

Epping Forest Yacht & Country Club is built around the historic duPont Mansion on the banks of the St. Johns River at 1830 Epping Forest Drive in Jacksonville — and it makes an impression the moment you arrive. Spanish moss, manicured English Gardens, and wide river views create a setting that's genuinely hard to find anywhere else in Northeast Florida. The property scales well, too: guest counts can range from an intimate dinner for 10 to an outdoor celebration of 600.

What separates Epping Forest from most Jacksonville venues is the ability to move through three distinct spaces in one evening — garden ceremony, terrace cocktail hour, mansion reception — all on the same property, with no shuttle and no venue change mid-night.

Why Couples Choose Epping Forest

  • Riverfront setting: The terraces and English Gardens look directly out over the St. Johns River — the kind of backdrop that shows up in wedding photos for decades and that guests still talk about long after the night is over.
  • Historic character: The duPont Mansion's original woodwork, high ceilings, and old-Florida architecture give indoor receptions a visual richness that modern hotel ballrooms simply can't replicate.
  • Seamless flow through the property: Ceremony in the gardens, cocktail hour on the terrace, dinner and dancing inside the mansion — no buses, no gap in the evening, no guests getting lost between venues.
  • In-house event coordinator: The venue assigns a dedicated coordinator to your wedding who manages room logistics and serves as the single point of contact for all your outside vendors, including your DJ, photographer, and caterer.

How a Typical Wedding Day Flows Here

Most Epping Forest weddings move through the property in a natural progression. The ceremony typically takes place in the English Gardens or on the riverfront terraces, where guests face the St. Johns River. After the ceremony, cocktail hour moves out to the terrace while the wedding party wraps portraits. The reception — dinner, toasts, and dancing — takes place inside one of the mansion's rooms, most often the Dupont & Mason Room or the full second floor for larger guest counts.

That flow means three distinct entertainment setups across the evening: amplified sound for the outdoor ceremony, background music during the cocktail hour, and a full DJ and dance floor setup for the reception inside. We map all three phases before your wedding day so each transition is smooth and the timing holds throughout the night.

An outdoor ceremony in the English Gardens needs a wireless microphone system and speakers positioned for open-air coverage. Cocktail hour on the terrace is ambient, background-level sound. The reception inside is a full setup — subwoofer, dance floor speakers, uplighting, and a DJ booth configured for the room. We account for all three phases in the planning process so there's nothing improvised on the day.
