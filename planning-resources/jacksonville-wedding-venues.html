Jacksonville is Florida's largest city by land area, and its wedding venues span the whole map — riverfront country clubs in Ortega and Southside, a working farm on the Westside, a cluster of urban and high-rise venues downtown and in Riverside, and a handful of country clubs and plantation-style venues further into Southside near Baymeadows. As a Jacksonville-based wedding DJ company that works across the entire city, here are fourteen venues that come up most often when couples ask us for recommendations.

01 Southside

Epping Forest Yacht & Country Club

A historic mansion on the St. Johns River with English Gardens and riverfront terraces, offering rooms from an intimate 30-guest Forest Room up to a 600-guest English Gardens ceremony. One of Jacksonville's most consistently booked wedding venues, and a property Footloose is a preferred vendor at.

efyc.com·(904) 739-7200

Read our full Epping Forest guide →
02 Ortega

Timuquana Country Club

A private riverfront club in Jacksonville's Ortega neighborhood with more than a century of history, offering its largest space for up to 300 seated guests or 400 for a cocktail-style reception, plus smaller rooms — the River Room seats 60, the Board Room seats 20 — for more intimate rehearsal dinners or bridal events.

timuquana.net·(904) 388-2664

03 Westside

Old Spanish Quarter

A 95-acre working farm on Jacksonville's west side with indoor and outdoor ceremony and reception space, seating up to 150 guests or hosting 200 for a cocktail-style event. A genuinely rural option that's still a practical drive from most of Jacksonville.

oldspanishquarter.com·(904) 257-3089

04 Downtown

The Lark

A downtown event space on the corner of North Hogan Street and West Monroe Street, hosting up to 150 guests for a ceremony, reception, or rehearsal dinner, or up to 214 for cocktail-style engagement parties and events — a flexible, centrally located option for couples who want a true downtown address.

thelarkjax.com·(904) 329-1992

05 Downtown

Brick and Beam

A 2,300-square-foot event space in Jacksonville's Historic Springfield District, seating up to 150 guests with flexible layouts for smaller, more intimate gatherings or larger cocktail-style celebrations — an urban-edge option just north of downtown proper.

brickandbeamjax.com

06 Downtown

The Library (Conference Center at the Main Library)

20,000 square feet of event space inside Jacksonville's Main Library downtown, offering a genuinely unique backdrop for couples who want a distinctive civic-building setting rather than a hotel ballroom or industrial warehouse.

jaxpubliclibrary.org·(904) 255-6198

07 Riverside

Garden Club of Jacksonville

A historic Riverside facility on the St. Johns River with a Grand Ballroom seating up to 350 for dining or 500 theater-style beneath six crystal chandeliers, plus the more intimate Clubroom for up to 50 seated — a strong option for couples who want elegant, neutral decor already in place.

gardenclubjax.org·(904) 355-4224

08 Riverside

Cummer Museum of Art & Gardens

A riverfront art museum in Riverside with historically significant gardens and St. Johns River sunset views, seating 160–220 guests. Voted best place to get married in Folio's Best of Jax, with an on-site caterer and both indoor and outdoor ceremony options.

cummermuseum.org·(904) 356-6857

09 Downtown

The River Club

A private club on the 34th and 35th floors of Riverplace Tower with sweeping views of downtown and the St. Johns River. Smaller rooms — the Ortega River Room, Duval Room, Avondale Room, and Mayport Room — accommodate 2 to 200 guests, while the River Ballroom and Florida Ballroom scale up to 350, giving couples a wide range of guest counts within one building.

jaxriverclub.com·(904) 354-1111

10 Downtown

Hyatt Regency Jacksonville Riverfront

A full-service downtown hotel directly on the St. Johns River, with a Grand Ballroom accommodating up to 1,900 guests seated or 3,200 for a cocktail reception, plus River Terrace Rooms for 450–600 guests — the largest-capacity option in this roundup by a wide margin, and a strong fit for large weddings with out-of-town guests who need on-site hotel rooms.

hyatt.com

11 Southside

Deerwood Country Club

A private country club in Jacksonville's Southside/Baymeadows area with a 25,000-square-foot clubhouse, seating roughly 50–200 guests for a plated dinner and accommodating parties up to 400 depending on setup. A reliable, full-service option for couples who want a traditional country-club backdrop.

deerwoodclub.com

12 Southside

Deercreek Country Club

A colonial-style clubhouse and landscaped veranda in Southside Jacksonville, with capacity ranging from around 150 seated guests up to 250 in the Clubhouse Ballroom for dining and dancing — a flexible option for both mid-size and larger receptions.

deercreekcountryclub.com·(904) 363-1604

13 Southside

Bowing Oaks Plantation

A Southside Jacksonville venue with a 3,200-square-foot air-conditioned ballroom seating up to 200, plus an open-air ceremony site beneath mature oak trees and a covered outdoor pavilion, each also accommodating up to 200 guests — a plantation-style option that pairs indoor and outdoor space on one property.

bowingoaks.com·(904) 600-5172

14 Southside

Deerwood Castle

A large-scale event venue in Southside Jacksonville with over 50,000 square feet of flexible space, headlined by a Grand Hall that seats 500-plus guests and can expand to host parties of 850 or more. The outdoor "Under the Oaks" ceremony space accommodates up to 300 guests — one of the largest-capacity venues in this entire roundup, and a strong fit for big weddings and galas.

deerwoodcastle.com·(904) 478-5530

Choosing Among These Venues

With fourteen venues spanning Southside, Ortega, Westside, Downtown, and Riverside, the right fit usually comes down to which part of the city is most convenient for you and your guests, and how large your wedding is. Epping Forest and Timuquana Country Club both sit on the St. Johns River with a country-club or historic-mansion feel, while Old Spanish Quarter offers a genuinely rural, farm-based setting on the west side. Downtown venues like The River Club, the Hyatt Regency, The Lark, and Brick and Beam trade that suburban riverfront setting for an urban, high-rise or industrial feel, while Garden Club of Jacksonville and the Cummer Museum bring riverfront gardens into the Riverside neighborhood just outside downtown proper. Further into Southside near Baymeadows, Deerwood Country Club, Deercreek Country Club, Bowing Oaks Plantation, and Deerwood Castle round things out with a mix of traditional country-club ballrooms, a plantation-style property, and one of the largest-capacity event spaces in the city.

Guest count varies widely across this list, from The Lark and Brick and Beam's roughly 150-guest range up to the Hyatt Regency's 1,900-guest ballroom and Deerwood Castle's 850-plus capacity Grand Hall. If you're planning a large wedding with 300 or more guests, Timuquana Country Club, The River Club, Deerwood Country Club, Deerwood Castle, and the Hyatt Regency are your strongest options; for more intimate weddings under 150 guests, The Lark and Brick and Beam tend to work better.

Booking timelines across Jacksonville track with the rest of Northeast Florida — popular fall and spring Saturdays at the most established venues can book out well over a year in advance during peak wedding season (roughly October through April), so it's worth reaching out early if you have a specific date in mind.

Planning entertainment across Jacksonville: riverfront estates and country clubs typically combine an outdoor ceremony lawn with an indoor reception space, downtown high-rises come with freight-elevator load-in and their own AV infrastructure, and historic mansions and museums often have specific power and placement restrictions to protect the building. As a Jacksonville-based DJ company, we plan sound and load-in around whichever venue and neighborhood you book.
