Best Orange Park Wedding Venues

A roundup of Orange Park wedding venues and wedding venues near Orange Park couples ask about most — wedding venues Orange Park FL, Clay County wedding venues, and Green Cove Springs wedding venues, from riverfront estates to historic mansions and rural barns.

Orange Park and Green Cove Springs sit just southwest of Jacksonville along the St. Johns River, and together they've quietly become one of Northeast Florida's richest corridors for wedding venues — riverfront estates, historic mansions, a converted Art Deco theater, and a couple of genuine barns, all within about a 20-minute drive of each other. As a wedding DJ company that works this corridor regularly, here are seven venues that come up most often when couples ask us for recommendations.

01

Club Continental

A historic riverside resort built as the Palmolive soap family's winter estate, with an oak grove ceremony lawn and 300-year-old oak tree draped in Spanish moss overlooking the St. Johns River. Full or half club rentals accommodate up to 200 or 120 guests, with on-site guest rooms and catering.

clubcontinental.com·(904) 264-6070

Read our full Club Continental guide →
02

Azaleana Manor

A fully renovated 10,000-square-foot mansion on four acres with more than 325 feet of St. Johns River frontage in Orange Park. Seats more than 150 guests inside, with room for 200-plus on the riverside lawn — a strong fit for couples who want historic architecture without sacrificing river views.

azaleanamanor.com·(904) 887-1250

03

Winterbourne Inn

A waterfront venue on the St. Johns River in Orange Park that books just one event at a time, so your wedding day gets the staff's undivided attention. Accommodates around 100 guests seated indoors, or up to 120 using both indoor and outdoor space — a good fit for couples planning a more intimate riverfront wedding.

winterbourneinn.com

04

The Hilltop Club

A historic Victorian mansion in the heart of Orange Park that's operated as a fine-dining restaurant and wedding venue for more than 40 years, with lush gardens, two outdoor ceremony areas, and five reception rooms. Scales from intimate gatherings of 10 up to receptions of 400, making it one of the more flexible venues in the area on guest count.

hilltop-club.com

05

The Manor at 12 Oaks

A 42-acre equestrian estate in Green Cove Springs, about 20 minutes south of Orange Park, with a lakeside ceremony site accommodating up to 300 guests and a Pavilion reception space seating up to 110. A popular option for couples in Middleburg, Fleming Island, and the greater Orange Park area who want a rural estate feel.

themanor12oaks.com·(904) 657-2290

06

The Clay Theatre

A restored 1937 Art Deco theater in downtown Green Cove Springs, accommodating up to 250 guests — a distinctive, non-traditional option for couples who want a vintage architectural backdrop rather than a mansion, barn, or ballroom.

claytheatre.com·(904) 419-7094

07

Chapel Oaks

A hand-built, two-story pine barn on 8 acres in Green Cove Springs, offering both indoor and outdoor ceremony and reception options — a genuinely rustic choice for couples who want open land and barn architecture further from the river corridor.

chapeloaksvenue.com·(828) 226-0700

Choosing Among These Venues

With seven venues spanning riverfront estates, historic mansions and theaters, and rural barns, the right fit usually comes down to how much river view matters to you, how large your guest list is, and how far south of Jacksonville you're willing to have guests drive.

If river views are the priority, Club Continental, Azaleana Manor, and Winterbourne Inn all sit directly on the St. Johns River, each with a different feel — Club Continental's oak grove ceremony lawn, Azaleana Manor's fully renovated mansion, and Winterbourne's single-event-at-a-time intimacy. For couples who want historic architecture without necessarily needing to be on the water, The Hilltop Club's Victorian mansion and The Clay Theatre's restored 1937 Art Deco interior both offer distinctive, non-ballroom backdrops.

Guest count varies widely across this list. The Hilltop Club's range from 10 to 400 guests and The Manor at 12 Oaks' 300-guest lakeside ceremony site make them strong choices for larger weddings, while Winterbourne Inn's more intimate scale and single-event booking suit smaller guest lists better. The Manor at 12 Oaks and Chapel Oaks both offer genuinely rural, barn-and-farmland settings for couples who want open space rather than a formal estate or ballroom — and both sit further south in Green Cove Springs, worth weighing if a shorter drive for Jacksonville-based guests matters to you.

Booking timelines in this corridor track closely with the rest of Northeast Florida — popular fall and spring Saturdays at the more established venues like Club Continental can book out well over a year in advance during peak wedding season (roughly October through April), so it's worth reaching out early if you have a specific date in mind.

Planning entertainment across these venues: riverfront estates like Club Continental and Azaleana Manor typically combine an outdoor ceremony lawn with an indoor reception space, which means two different sound setups in one day, while venues like The Clay Theatre and The Hilltop Club have more fixed, purpose-built interiors. As a Jacksonville-area wedding DJ company, we plan sound and load-in around whichever venue and space combination you book.
