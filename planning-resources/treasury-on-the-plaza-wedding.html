Treasury on the Plaza Wedding Guide

A 1920s bank vault turned cocktail bar, exclusive use of a historic downtown building, and a package structure built for weddings of 40 to 175 — here's what to know before booking Treasury on the Plaza in St. Augustine.

About Treasury on the Plaza

Treasury on the Plaza sits at 24 Cathedral Place in the heart of downtown St. Augustine, inside a building that opened as a bank in the 1920s. An award-winning renovation turned it into one of the city's most distinctive wedding venues — preserving original details like the bank vault while updating the space with modern event infrastructure. The result is a venue that feels genuinely historic rather than merely themed, which is rare even in a city with as much architectural history as St. Augustine.

Treasury on the Plaza books exclusive-use weddings only and is part of the Treasury Venue Collection. When you're booked, the entire building is yours for the day — no other event, no shared spaces, no strangers in the hallway during your reception.

Packages & Capacity

Treasury on the Plaza hosts weddings from 40 to 175 guests, and offers four main package tiers rather than an a la carte room rental:

PackageBest For
Intimate Wedding Package40–100 guests, all-inclusive for smaller weddings
Custom Wedding Package40–175 guests, choose your own vendors
Eat, Drink & Be Married100–175 guests, venue/food/coordination/bar included
All-Inclusive Package100–175 guests, most vendors bundled in

The venue notes it works best for weddings in the 75–150 guest range — large enough to feel celebratory, small enough that the historic space doesn't feel empty. Couples planning a smaller wedding under 100 guests should ask about the Intimate Wedding Package specifically, since it's built around that guest range rather than scaled down from the larger packages.

Why Couples Choose Treasury on the Plaza

  • The Vault: The original 1920s bank vault — now a bar and cocktail focal point — is unlike anything else in St. Augustine's wedding venue landscape. It photographs distinctively and gives guests something to remember beyond the standard reception setup.
  • Exclusive use, full building: When you book Treasury, the entire historic building is yours for the day. No shared lobby, no other couple's guests in your cocktail hour, no risk of noise bleed from an event next door.
  • Downtown walkability: Steps from St. Augustine's historic plaza and Cathedral Basilica — guests staying nearby can walk to the venue, and post-ceremony photos in the plaza are easy without a transportation plan.
  • Package structure that actually helps: Most venues charge by the hour and layer on vendor costs separately. Treasury's package tiers bundle in catering, coordination, and bar options — which simplifies planning significantly for couples who don't want to source every vendor independently.

How a Typical Wedding Day Flows Here

Most Treasury on the Plaza weddings use the main floor for both the ceremony and reception — the exclusive-use booking means the space can be configured for ceremony, then reset for dining and dancing without guests having to move between buildings. The Vault typically serves as the cocktail hour focal point while the main floor is turned over for reception, which works naturally given The Vault's intimate, bar-like atmosphere.

Because it's an indoor historic building with a fixed footprint rather than a flexible ballroom, your entertainment setup needs to work within the existing architecture. That means sound coverage planned around the room's dimensions and acoustics, positioning that accounts for The Vault area versus the main reception floor, and load-in logistics coordinated downtown. We walk through all of this before your wedding day so the setup is right for the specific configuration you've booked — not a generic ballroom approach dropped into a 1920s bank building.

Planning Your Entertainment

Footloose Entertainment provides Treasury on the Plaza wedding entertainment for couples throughout St. Augustine, and as a Treasury Plaza St Augustine DJ choice for couples who want experience in this specific building, we plan the setup around the venue's actual architecture rather than adapting a generic ballroom configuration. The fixed indoor footprint, the acoustic distinction between The Vault and the main floor, and the downtown load-in logistics all factor into how we plan the day.

The most important thing to confirm before your wedding day is how you're using the two spaces — whether The Vault is cocktail-hour-only or part of the reception itself determines the sound and speaker setup for that area. The main reception floor needs full coverage for dinner and dancing. Getting both right means planning them specifically, not assuming one setup works for both.

What we confirm before every Treasury on the Plaza wedding: your package tier and what's included, whether The Vault is in use during the reception or cocktail hour only, downtown load-in access and timing, and power access for the configuration you've booked.
